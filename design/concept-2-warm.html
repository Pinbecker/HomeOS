Concept 2 — Warm

Design notes

Feel

Like a notebook you actually want to pick up. Warm cream tones and terracotta accents give it personality without being loud. Feels personal, not corporate.

Typography

Lora serif for headers (warmth, personality) and Inter for body (clean readability). The serif gives section titles a proper home, not a software app.

Colour story

Terracotta (active, primary), sage green (completion, positive), warm brown (text), cream (background). Amber for warnings. Each colour has one meaning.

For Imogen

Most likely to feel like something she chose herself rather than something IT installed. The mood picker and film suggestions feel inviting rather than functional.

Trade-offs

Requires a Google Fonts load (Lora). Slightly heavier than concept 1. The warm palette can feel dated if not implemented carefully. Needs consistent photography/imagery to shine in the film grid.

Best recommendation

This is the strongest concept for the stated goal: a shared home that doesn't feel like admin software. The film grid with poster colours proves the concept immediately.
