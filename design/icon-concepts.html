HomeOS — App Icon Concepts

Five directions with an iOS 26 “liquid glass” feel: translucent layered panels, specular sheen, edge light. Big = home-screen size; the two small tiles test legibility on light and dark wallpapers. All built on the app palette (accent blue / red / sage / amber / indigo).

Glass Hearth

1 · Glass Hearth

The familiar house glyph, but built from stacked frosted-glass panels over a warm amber→red gradient. Reads instantly as “home,” the warm tones say hearth not utility. Closest to the current icon, just elevated.

Together

2 · Together

Two translucent house shapes — Dan & Imogen — overlapping like Photos’ petals, blending to teal where they meet. White frosted background = the modern glass look. Symbolic, personal, and unmistakably shared.

Pinwheel

3 · Pinwheel

The most direct Photos homage: five overlapping glass petals, one per nav section (Watch / Household / Inbox / Reminders / Notes), blending where they cross. Colourful and joyful — but more abstract, doesn’t literally say “home.”

Clear Glass

4 · Clear Glass House

The literal “liquid glass” variant: a near-transparent house cut from frosted glass, edge-lit with refraction streaks behind it, over a cool blue tint. Most faithful to iOS 26’s clear material — calm and premium, less bold from a distance.

Warm Window

5 · Warm Window

“Coming home” at dusk: a glowing doorway spilling warm light against a deep indigo glass. The most emotional, cosy direction — leans hard into warm & personal. Striking on a dark wallpaper; the glyph is more illustrative than iconic.
